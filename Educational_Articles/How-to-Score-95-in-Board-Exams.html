How to Score 95%+ in Board Exams: Complete Study Strategy & Daily Study Plan

Scoring 95% or more in board exams is a dream for many students. The good news is that achieving this goal does not require extraordinary intelligence. It requires smart planning, consistent effort, regular revision, and disciplined study habits. Every year, thousands of students score above 95% because they follow a proper study strategy instead of relying on last-minute preparation. In this article, you will learn practical tips and a complete study plan to help you perform your best in board examinations.

1. Set a Clear Goal

The first step towards success is setting a clear target. Instead of saying, "I want good marks," decide the exact percentage you want to score. Divide your target into subject-wise goals. For example:

Subject Target Marks
Mathematics 100
Science 98
Social Science 95
English 95
Hindi 95

A clear goal keeps you motivated and helps you measure your progress.

2. Complete NCERT Thoroughly

NCERT textbooks are the foundation of board exam preparation. Read every chapter carefully, solve all exercise questions, understand examples, and revise important definitions, formulas, diagrams, and summary points. Many board examination questions are directly or indirectly based on NCERT concepts.

3. Make a Daily Study Timetable

A proper timetable helps you study regularly without feeling stressed.

Time Activity
5:30 AM - 7:00 AM Mathematics or Numericals
7:00 AM - 7:30 AM Revision
After School Homework and Today's Revision
5:00 PM - 7:00 PM Science
7:30 PM - 9:00 PM English / Social Science
9:00 PM - 9:30 PM Quick Revision

Study consistently every day instead of studying for very long hours only before exams.

4. Revise Regularly

Revision is the key to remembering concepts for a long time. Follow this simple revision cycle:

  • First Revision – Within 24 hours.
  • Second Revision – After one week.
  • Third Revision – After one month.

Regular revision reduces forgetting and improves confidence.

5. Solve Previous Year Question Papers

Previous year papers help you understand the exam pattern, important topics, marking scheme, and time management. Solve at least the last 5–10 years of board papers and official sample papers. Practice writing answers within the allotted time.

6. Practice Writing Answers

Reading alone is not enough. Practice writing long answers, short answers, numerical problems, diagrams, and maps. Writing improves speed, presentation, and accuracy, which are essential for scoring high marks.

7. Subject-wise Preparation Strategy

Mathematics

  • Practice questions every day.
  • Memorize formulas.
  • Solve NCERT completely.
  • Attempt difficult questions repeatedly.

Science

  • Understand concepts instead of memorizing.
  • Learn formulas and chemical equations.
  • Practice diagrams regularly.
  • Solve numerical questions daily.

Social Science

  • Read chapters carefully.
  • Prepare short notes.
  • Learn important dates and events.
  • Practice map work.

English and Hindi

  • Improve grammar.
  • Practice writing skills.
  • Read textbook chapters carefully.
  • Learn important questions and answers.

8. Improve Answer Presentation

Presentation creates a good impression on the examiner. Follow these tips:

  • Write neatly.
  • Underline important keywords.
  • Leave proper spacing.
  • Draw labelled diagrams where required.
  • Write answers point-wise whenever possible.

9. Stay Healthy

A healthy body supports better learning. Sleep for 7–8 hours, eat nutritious food, drink enough water, and take short breaks while studying. Avoid studying continuously for many hours without rest.

10. Last Month Preparation

  • Complete the syllabus before the last month.
  • Revise all subjects thoroughly.
  • Solve full-length mock tests.
  • Focus on weak topics.
  • Revise formulas, diagrams, and important definitions daily.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Ignoring NCERT textbooks.
  • Studying only before exams.
  • Not revising regularly.
  • Using mobile phones during study time.
  • Skipping previous year question papers.
  • Not practicing answer writing.

Final Tips for Success

  • Stay consistent every day.
  • Believe in yourself.
  • Study smart instead of studying for very long hours.
  • Ask teachers whenever you have doubts.
  • Keep a positive attitude throughout your preparation.
