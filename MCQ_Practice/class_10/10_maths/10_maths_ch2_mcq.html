CLASS 10 MATHEMATICS

Chapter 2: Polynomials

Note: These questions are designed based on NCERT concepts. Select the correct option for each question.

  1. If \( p(x) \) is a polynomial in \( x \), the highest power of \( x \) in \( p(x) \) is called the:
    a) Root
    b) Zero
    c) Degree
    d) Coefficient

  2. Which of the following is NOT a polynomial?
    a) \( 4x + 2 \)
    b) \( 2y^2 - 3y + 4 \)
    c) \( \frac{1}{x-1} \)
    d) \( 7u^6 - 4 \)

  3. A polynomial of degree 1 is called a:
    a) Constant polynomial
    b) Linear polynomial
    c) Quadratic polynomial
    d) Cubic polynomial

  4. The word ‘quadratic’ is derived from the word ‘quadrate’, which means:
    a) Four
    b) Square
    c) Root
    d) Product

  5. The general form of a quadratic polynomial in \( x \) is \( ax^2 + bx + c \), where \( a, b, c \) are real numbers and:
    a) \( a = 0 \)
    b) \( a \neq 0 \)
    c) \( b \neq 0 \)
    d) \( c \neq 0 \)

  6. A cubic polynomial has a degree of:
    a) 1
    b) 2
    c) 3
    d) 4

  7. If \( k \) is a real number such that \( p(k) = 0 \), then \( k \) is called a:
    a) Constant of the polynomial
    b) Degree of the polynomial
    c) Zero of the polynomial
    d) Factor of the coefficient

  8. The zero of the linear polynomial \( ax + b \) is related to its coefficients as:
    a) \( b/a \)
    b) \( -a/b \)
    c) \( -b/a \)
    d) \( ab \)

  9. Geometrically, the zeroes of a polynomial \( p(x) \) are the x-coordinates of the points where the graph of \( y = p(x) \) intersects the:
    a) Y-axis
    b) X-axis
    c) Origin
    d) Line \( y = x \)

  10. The graph of a linear polynomial \( y = ax + b \) is a:
    a) Parabola
    b) Hyperbola
    c) Straight line
    d) Circle

  11. The shape of the graph of a quadratic polynomial \( y = ax^2 + bx + c \) is called a:
    a) Linear curve
    b) Parabola
    c) Cubic curve
    d) Ellipse

  12. A parabola \( y = ax^2 + bx + c \) opens upwards if:
    a) \( a < 0 \)
    b) \( a > 0 \)
    c) \( a = 0 \)
    d) \( b > 0 \)

  13. The maximum number of zeroes a quadratic polynomial can have is:
    a) 1
    b) 2
    c) 3
    d) 0

  14. If the graph of a quadratic polynomial touches the x-axis at exactly one point, the polynomial has:
    a) Two distinct zeroes
    b) No real zero
    c) Two equal zeroes (one zero)
    d) Infinite zeroes

  15. If the graph of a quadratic polynomial is completely above the x-axis, the number of zeroes is:
    a) 1
    b) 2
    c) 0
    d) 3

  16. A polynomial of degree \( n \) can have at most:
    a) \( n+1 \) zeroes
    b) \( n-1 \) zeroes
    c) \( n \) zeroes
    d) 1 zero

  17. The zeroes of the quadratic polynomial \( x^2 - 3x - 4 \) are:
    a) 1 and 4
    b) -1 and 4
    c) -1 and -4
    d) 1 and -4

  18. For a quadratic polynomial \( ax^2 + bx + c \), the sum of the zeroes \( \alpha + \beta \) is:
    a) \( c/a \)
    b) \( b/a \)
    c) \( -b/a \)
    d) \( -c/a \)

  19. For a quadratic polynomial \( ax^2 + bx + c \), the product of the zeroes \( \alpha\beta \) is:
    a) \( -b/a \)
    b) \( c/a \)
    c) \( b/a \)
    d) \( -c/a \)

  20. If the sum and product of zeroes of a quadratic polynomial are \( -3 \) and \( 2 \) respectively, the polynomial is:
    a) \( x^2 - 3x + 2 \)
    b) \( x^2 + 3x + 2 \)
    c) \( x^2 + 3x - 2 \)
    d) \( x^2 - 3x - 2 \)

  21. The zeroes of the polynomial \( x^2 + 7x + 10 \) are:
    a) 2, 5
    b) -2, -5
    c) -2, 5
    d) 2, -5

  22. The zeroes of the polynomial \( x^2 - 3 \) are:
    a) 3, -3
    b) \( \sqrt{3}, -\sqrt{3} \)
    c) 3, 0
    d) \( \sqrt{3}, 0 \)

  23. The number of zeroes for the cubic polynomial \( y = x^3 - 4x \) is:
    a) 1
    b) 2
    c) 3
    d) 4

  24. For the cubic polynomial \( ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d \), the sum of the zeroes \( \alpha + \beta + \gamma \) is:
    a) \( c/a \)
    b) \( -b/a \)
    c) \( -d/a \)
    d) \( b/a \)

  25. For a cubic polynomial, the product of the zeroes \( \alpha\beta\gamma \) is:
    a) \( c/a \)
    b) \( -b/a \)
    c) \( -d/a \)
    d) \( d/a \)

  26. In a cubic polynomial, the sum of the products of zeroes taken two at a time (\( \alpha\beta + \beta\gamma + \gamma\alpha \)) is:
    a) \( -b/a \)
    b) \( c/a \)
    c) \( -d/a \)
    d) \( -c/a \)

  27. If a cubic polynomial is \( x^3 - x^2 \), its zeroes are:
    a) 1 only
    b) 0 only
    c) 0 and 1
    d) 0, 1, and -1

  28. The number of zeroes of a polynomial whose graph is parallel to the x-axis and does not touch it is:
    a) 1
    b) 0
    c) Infinite
    d) 2

  29. Competency-Based: A student observes that the graph of a polynomial intersects the x-axis at 4 distinct points. This polynomial must have a degree of at least:
    a) 3
    b) 4
    c) 5
    d) 2

  30. The constant term of a linear polynomial \( ax + b \) is \( b \). Its zero is:
    a) \( \frac{\text{Constant term}}{\text{Coefficient of } x} \)
    b) \( -\frac{\text{Constant term}}{\text{Coefficient of } x} \)
    c) \( \frac{\text{Coefficient of } x}{\text{Constant term}} \)
    d) \( -\frac{\text{Coefficient of } x}{\text{Constant term}} \)

  31. If \( \alpha, \beta \) are the zeroes of \( 2x^2 - 8x + 6 \), then \( \alpha + \beta \) is:
    a) 4
    b) -4
    c) 3
    d) -3

  32. The product of zeroes of the polynomial \( 3x^2 + 5x - 2 \) is:
    a) \( 5/3 \)
    b) \( -5/3 \)
    c) \( -2/3 \)
    d) \( 2/3 \)

  33. Which of the following polynomials has \( -2 \) and \( 4 \) as its zeroes?
    a) \( x^2 - 2x - 8 \)
    b) \( x^2 + 2x - 8 \)
    c) \( x^2 - 2x + 8 \)
    d) \( x^2 + 2x + 8 \)

  34. A quadratic polynomial whose sum of zeroes is \( 0 \) and one zero is \( 3 \) is:
    a) \( x^2 - 9 \)
    b) \( x^2 + 9 \)
    c) \( x^2 - 3 \)
    d) \( x^2 + 3 \)

  35. In the cubic polynomial \( p(x) = 3x^3 - 5x^2 - 11x - 3 \), the value of \( \alpha\beta\gamma \) is:
    a) 1
    b) -1
    c) 3
    d) -3

  36. If a polynomial \( p(x) \) has zeroes \( 4, -2, \) and \( 1/2 \), it is most likely a:
    a) Linear polynomial
    b) Quadratic polynomial
    c) Cubic polynomial
    d) Bi-quadratic polynomial

  37. Competency-Based: If the graph of \( y = p(x) \) passes through the origin, then one of the zeroes of \( p(x) \) is:
    a) 1
    b) -1
    c) 0
    d) Cannot be determined

  38. The degree of the polynomial \( 5x^3 - 4x^2 + x - 2 \) is:
    a) 2
    b) 3
    c) 1
    d) 0

  39. A quadratic polynomial can have at most how many points of intersection with the x-axis?
    a) 0
    b) 1
    c) 2
    d) 3

  40. The zeroes of the polynomial \( 4u^2 + 8u \) are:
    a) 0, 2
    b) 0, -2
    c) 4, 8
    d) -4, -8

  41. If \( \alpha + \beta = 4 \) and \( \alpha\beta = 1 \) for a quadratic polynomial, the polynomial is:
    a) \( x^2 - 4x + 1 \)
    b) \( x^2 + 4x + 1 \)
    c) \( x^2 - 4x - 1 \)
    d) \( x^2 + 4x - 1 \)

  42. The sum of the zeroes of the cubic polynomial \( 2x^3 - 5x^2 - 14x + 8 \) is:
    a) \( -5/2 \)
    b) \( 5/2 \)
    c) \( 7 \)
    d) \( -7 \)

  43. If one zero of the quadratic polynomial \( ax^2 + bx + c \) is the reciprocal of the other, then:
    a) \( a = b \)
    b) \( b = c \)
    c) \( a = c \)
    d) \( a = 0 \)

  44. Competency-Based: For which value of \( a \) will the graph of \( y = ax^2 + bx + c \) be a downward parabola?
    a) \( a = 5 \)
    b) \( a = 0 \)
    c) \( a = -3 \)
    d) \( a = 1 \)

  45. The degree of a constant polynomial (like \( p(x) = 5 \)) is:
    a) 1
    b) 0
    c) Undefined
    d) 2

  46. If the zeroes of a quadratic polynomial are \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \), then the polynomial can be written as:
    a) \( k[x^2 + (\alpha+\beta)x + \alpha\beta] \)
    b) \( k[x^2 - (\alpha+\beta)x + \alpha\beta] \)
    c) \( k[x^2 - (\alpha+\beta)x - \alpha\beta] \)
    d) \( k[x^2 + (\alpha+\beta)x - \alpha\beta] \)

  47. The zeroes of the polynomial \( t^2 - 15 \) are:
    a) 15, -15
    b) \( \sqrt{15}, -\sqrt{15} \)
    c) 0, 15
    d) \( \sqrt{15}, 0 \)

  48. In the quadratic polynomial \( 4s^2 - 4s + 1 \), the sum of zeroes is:
    a) 1
    b) -1
    c) 1/4
    d) 4

  49. A cubic polynomial has zeroes \( \alpha, \beta, \gamma \). If \( \alpha + \beta + \gamma = 2 \), \( \alpha\beta + \beta\gamma + \gamma\alpha = -7 \), and \( \alpha\beta\gamma = -14 \), the polynomial is:
    a) \( x^3 - 2x^2 - 7x + 14 \)
    b) \( x^3 - 2x^2 - 7x - 14 \)
    c) \( x^3 + 2x^2 - 7x + 14 \)
    d) \( x^3 - 2x^2 + 7x + 14 \)

  50. If a polynomial has no real zeroes, its graph:
    a) Intersects the x-axis at two points
    b) Touches the x-axis at one point
    c) Does not intersect the x-axis at all
    d) Is a straight line through the origin
