Question 1

Find the transpose of each of the following matrices.

(i)

The given matrix is

$$ A=\begin{bmatrix} 5\\ \dfrac{1}{2}\\ -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

We know that the transpose of a matrix is obtained by interchanging its rows and columns.

Here, the given matrix is a column matrix having three rows and one column. Therefore, its transpose will be a row matrix having one row and three columns.

$$ A^T=\begin{bmatrix} 5 & \dfrac{1}{2} & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Hence, the transpose of the given matrix is

$$ \boxed{ A^T=\begin{bmatrix} 5 & \dfrac{1}{2} & -1 \end{bmatrix} } $$

(ii)

The given matrix is

$$ A=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1\\ 2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} $$

To find the transpose, interchange the rows and columns of the matrix.

Thus, the first row becomes the first column and the second row becomes the second column.

$$ A=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1\\ 2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ \Downarrow $$ $$ A^T=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2\\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Hence, the transpose of the given matrix is

$$ \boxed{ A^T=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2\\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} } $$

(iii)

The given matrix is

$$ A=\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 5 & 6\\ \sqrt3 & 5 & 6\\ 2 & 3 & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

To obtain the transpose, interchange the rows and columns of the matrix.

Hence, the first row becomes the first column, the second row becomes the second column and the third row becomes the third column.

$$ A=\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 5 & 6\\ \sqrt3 & 5 & 6\\ 2 & 3 & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ \Downarrow $$ $$ A^T=\begin{bmatrix} -1 & \sqrt3 & 2\\ 5 & 5 & 3\\ 6 & 6 & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Hence, the transpose of the given matrix is

$$ \boxed{ A^T=\begin{bmatrix} -1 & \sqrt3 & 2\\ 5 & 5 & 3\\ 6 & 6 & -1 \end{bmatrix} } $$

Question 2

If

$$ A=\begin{bmatrix} -1&2&3\\ 5&7&9\\ -2&1&1 \end{bmatrix} \quad\text{and}\quad B=\begin{bmatrix} -4&1&-5\\ 1&2&0\\ 1&3&1 \end{bmatrix}, $$

verify that

(i) $(A+B)^T=A^T+B^T$

First, find $A+B$.

$$ A+B= \begin{bmatrix} -1+(-4) & 2+1 & 3+(-5)\\ 5+1 & 7+2 & 9+0\\ -2+1 & 1+3 & 1+1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A+B= \begin{bmatrix} -5&3&-2\\ 6&9&9\\ -1&4&2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Taking transpose,

$$ (A+B)^T= \begin{bmatrix} -5&6&-1\\ 3&9&4\\ -2&9&2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now, find $A^T$ and $B^T$ separately.

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1&5&-2\\ 2&7&1\\ 3&9&1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ B^T= \begin{bmatrix} -4&1&1\\ 1&2&3\\ -5&0&1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Add these matrices.

$$ A^T+B^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1+(-4)&5+1&-2+1\\ 2+1&7+2&1+3\\ 3+(-5)&9+0&1+1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A^T+B^T= \begin{bmatrix} -5&6&-1\\ 3&9&4\\ -2&9&2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ (A+B)^T= A^T+B^T= \begin{bmatrix} -5&6&-1\\ 3&9&4\\ -2&9&2 \end{bmatrix}, $$

Hence, $(A+B)^T=A^T+B^T$ is verified.

(ii)

Verify that

$$ (A-B)^T=A^T-B^T $$

First, calculate $A-B$.

$$ A-B= \begin{bmatrix} -1-(-4)&2-1&3-(-5)\\ 5-1&7-2&9-0\\ -2-1&1-3&1-1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A-B= \begin{bmatrix} 3&1&8\\ 4&5&9\\ -3&-2&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Taking transpose,

$$ (A-B)^T= \begin{bmatrix} 3&4&-3\\ 1&5&-2\\ 8&9&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now subtract $B^T$ from $A^T$.

$$ A^T-B^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1-(-4)&5-1&-2-1\\ 2-1&7-2&1-3\\ 3-(-5)&9-0&1-1 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A^T-B^T= \begin{bmatrix} 3&4&-3\\ 1&5&-2\\ 8&9&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ (A-B)^T= A^T-B^T= \begin{bmatrix} 3&4&-3\\ 1&5&-2\\ 8&9&0 \end{bmatrix}, $$

Hence, $(A-B)^T=A^T-B^T$ is verified.

Question 3

Given

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 3&4\\ -1&2\\ 0&1 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad B= \begin{bmatrix} -1&2&1\\ 1&2&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Find matrix $A$.

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 3&-1&0\\ 4&2&1 \end{bmatrix} $$

(i)

Verify that

$$ (A+B)^T=A^T+B^T $$

First, calculate $A+B$.

$$ A+B= \begin{bmatrix} 3+(-1)&-1+2&0+1\\ 4+1&2+2&1+3 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A+B= \begin{bmatrix} 2&1&1\\ 5&4&4 \end{bmatrix} $$

Taking transpose,

$$ (A+B)^T= \begin{bmatrix} 2&5\\ 1&4\\ 1&4 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now find $B^T$.

$$ B^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1&1\\ 2&2\\ 1&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Add $A^T$ and $B^T$.

$$ A^T+B^T= \begin{bmatrix} 3+(-1)&4+1\\ -1+2&2+2\\ 0+1&1+3 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A^T+B^T= \begin{bmatrix} 2&5\\ 1&4\\ 1&4 \end{bmatrix} $$

Therefore,

$$ (A+B)^T=A^T+B^T $$

Hence verified.

(ii)

Verify that

$$ (A-B)^T=A^T-B^T $$

First, calculate $A-B$.

$$ A-B= \begin{bmatrix} 3-(-1)&-1-2&0-1\\ 4-1&2-2&1-3 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A-B= \begin{bmatrix} 4&-3&-1\\ 3&0&-2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Taking transpose,

$$ (A-B)^T= \begin{bmatrix} 4&3\\ -3&0\\ -1&-2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now subtract $B^T$ from $A^T$.

$$ A^T-B^T= \begin{bmatrix} 3-(-1)&4-1\\ -1-2&2-2\\ 0-1&1-3 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A^T-B^T= \begin{bmatrix} 4&3\\ -3&0\\ -1&-2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ (A-B)^T=A^T-B^T, $$

Hence, the given relation is verified.

Question 4

If

$$ A^T=\begin{bmatrix} -2&3\\ 1&2 \end{bmatrix} \quad\text{and}\quad B=\begin{bmatrix} -1&0\\ 1&2 \end{bmatrix}, $$

find $(A+2B)^T$.

Solution:

Since the transpose of matrix $A$ is given, first find matrix $A$.

$$ A=\begin{bmatrix} -2&1\\ 3&2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now, multiply matrix $B$ by $2$.

$$ 2B=2\begin{bmatrix} -1&0\\ 1&2 \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix} -2&0\\ 2&4 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now find $A+2B$.

$$ A+2B= \begin{bmatrix} -2&1\\ 3&2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -2&0\\ 2&4 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A+2B= \begin{bmatrix} -4&1\\ 5&6 \end{bmatrix} $$

Taking transpose of the above matrix, we get

$$ (A+2B)^T= \begin{bmatrix} -4&5\\ 1&6 \end{bmatrix} $$

Hence,

$$ \boxed{ (A+2B)^T= \begin{bmatrix} -4&5\\ 1&6 \end{bmatrix} } $$

Question 5 (i)

For the matrices $A$ and $B$, verify that

$$ (AB)^T=B^TA^T, $$

where

$$ A=\begin{bmatrix} 1\\ -4\\ 3 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad B=\begin{bmatrix} -1&2&1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Solution:

First, find the product $AB$.

$$ AB= \begin{bmatrix} 1\\ -4\\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -1&2&1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Multiply each element of the column matrix by every element of the row matrix.

$$ AB= \begin{bmatrix} 1(-1)&1(2)&1(1)\\ -4(-1)&-4(2)&-4(1)\\ 3(-1)&3(2)&3(1) \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ AB= \begin{bmatrix} -1&2&1\\ 4&-8&-4\\ -3&6&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now, take the transpose of $AB$.

$$ (AB)^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1&4&-3\\ 2&-8&6\\ 1&-4&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now, find the transpose of matrices $A$ and $B$ separately.

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 1&-4&3 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ B^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1\\ 2\\ 1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now calculate $B^TA^T$.

$$ B^TA^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1\\ 2\\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1&-4&3 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ B^TA^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1(1)&-1(-4)&-1(3)\\ 2(1)&2(-4)&2(3)\\ 1(1)&1(-4)&1(3) \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ B^TA^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1&4&-3\\ 2&-8&6\\ 1&-4&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ (AB)^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1&4&-3\\ 2&-8&6\\ 1&-4&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

and

$$ B^TA^T= \begin{bmatrix} -1&4&-3\\ 2&-8&6\\ 1&-4&3 \end{bmatrix}, $$

we have

$$ (AB)^T=B^TA^T. $$

Hence, the given relation is verified.

Question 6 (i)

If

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} \cos\alpha & \sin\alpha\\ -\sin\alpha & \cos\alpha \end{bmatrix}, $$

verify that

$$ A^TA=I. $$

Solution:

First, find the transpose of matrix $A$.

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} \cos\alpha & -\sin\alpha\\ \sin\alpha & \cos\alpha \end{bmatrix} $$

Now multiply $A^T$ and $A$.

$$ A^TA= \begin{bmatrix} \cos\alpha & -\sin\alpha\\ \sin\alpha & \cos\alpha \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \cos\alpha & \sin\alpha\\ -\sin\alpha & \cos\alpha \end{bmatrix} $$

Multiply the corresponding rows and columns.

$$ A^TA= \begin{bmatrix} \cos^2\alpha+\sin^2\alpha & \cos\alpha\sin\alpha-\sin\alpha\cos\alpha\\ \sin\alpha\cos\alpha-\cos\alpha\sin\alpha & \sin^2\alpha+\cos^2\alpha \end{bmatrix} $$

Using the identities

$$ \sin^2\alpha+\cos^2\alpha=1 $$

and

$$ \cos\alpha\sin\alpha-\sin\alpha\cos\alpha=0, $$

we get

$$ A^TA= \begin{bmatrix} 1&0\\ 0&1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ I= \begin{bmatrix} 1&0\\ 0&1 \end{bmatrix}, $$

therefore,

$$ A^TA=I. $$

Hence, the given relation is verified.

Question 7 (i)

Show that the matrix

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 1&-1&5\\ -1&2&1\\ 5&1&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

is a symmetric matrix.

Solution:

We know that a matrix is said to be symmetric if

$$ A^T=A. $$

First, find the transpose of the given matrix.

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 1&-1&5\\ -1&2&1\\ 5&1&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Comparing the matrices, we observe that

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 1&-1&5\\ -1&2&1\\ 5&1&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

and

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 1&-1&5\\ -1&2&1\\ 5&1&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ A^T=A, $$

the given matrix satisfies the condition of a symmetric matrix.

Hence, the given matrix is a symmetric matrix.

Question 7 (ii)

Show that the matrix

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 0&1&-1\\ -1&0&1\\ 1&-1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

is a skew-symmetric matrix.

Solution:

We know that a matrix is said to be skew-symmetric if

$$ A^T=-A. $$

First, find the transpose of the given matrix.

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 0&1&-1\\ -1&0&1\\ 1&-1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Interchanging the rows and columns, we get

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 0&-1&1\\ 1&0&-1\\ -1&1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now find the negative of matrix $A$ by multiplying each element by $-1$.

$$ -A= -\begin{bmatrix} 0&1&-1\\ -1&0&1\\ 1&-1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ -A= \begin{bmatrix} 0&-1&1\\ 1&0&-1\\ -1&1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Comparing the matrices, we have

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 0&-1&1\\ 1&0&-1\\ -1&1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

and

$$ -A= \begin{bmatrix} 0&-1&1\\ 1&0&-1\\ -1&1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ A^T=-A, $$

the given matrix satisfies the condition of a skew-symmetric matrix.

Hence, the given matrix is a skew-symmetric matrix.

Question 8

For the matrix

$$ A=\begin{bmatrix} 1&5\\ 6&7 \end{bmatrix}, $$

verify that

(i) $(A+A^T)$ is a symmetric matrix.

Solution:

First, find the transpose of matrix $A$.

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 1&6\\ 5&7 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now, find $A+A^T$.

$$ A+A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 1&5\\ 6&7 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 1&6\\ 5&7 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A+A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 2&11\\ 11&14 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now, take the transpose of $(A+A^T)$.

$$ (A+A^T)^T= \begin{bmatrix} 2&11\\ 11&14 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ (A+A^T)^T= A+A^T, $$

therefore, $(A+A^T)$ is a symmetric matrix.

Hence verified.

(ii)

Verify that

$$ (A-A^T) $$

is a skew-symmetric matrix.

Solution:

Using

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 1&5\\ 6&7 \end{bmatrix} \quad\text{and}\quad A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 1&6\\ 5&7 \end{bmatrix}, $$

we get

$$ A-A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 1&5\\ 6&7 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 1&6\\ 5&7 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A-A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 0&-1\\ 1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now, take the transpose of $(A-A^T)$.

$$ (A-A^T)^T= \begin{bmatrix} 0&1\\ -1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Also, find the negative of $(A-A^T)$.

$$ -(A-A^T)= - \begin{bmatrix} 0&-1\\ 1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ -(A-A^T)= \begin{bmatrix} 0&1\\ -1&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ (A-A^T)^T=-(A-A^T), $$

therefore, $(A-A^T)$ is a skew-symmetric matrix.

Hence verified.

Question 9

Find

$$ \frac{1}{2}(A+A^T) \quad\text{and}\quad \frac{1}{2}(A-A^T), $$

when

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 0&a&b\\ -a&0&c\\ -b&-c&0 \end{bmatrix}. $$

Solution:

First, find the transpose of matrix $A$.

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 0&-a&-b\\ a&0&-c\\ b&c&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now, calculate $A+A^T$.

$$ A+A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 0&a&b\\ -a&0&c\\ -b&-c&0 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 0&-a&-b\\ a&0&-c\\ b&c&0 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A+A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Therefore,

$$ \frac12(A+A^T) = \frac12 \begin{bmatrix} 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now, calculate $A-A^T$.

$$ A-A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 0&a&b\\ -a&0&c\\ -b&-c&0 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 0&-a&-b\\ a&0&-c\\ b&c&0 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A-A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 0&2a&2b\\ -2a&0&2c\\ -2b&-2c&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Hence,

$$ \frac12(A-A^T) = \frac12 \begin{bmatrix} 0&2a&2b\\ -2a&0&2c\\ -2b&-2c&0 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ \boxed{ \frac12(A-A^T) = \begin{bmatrix} 0&a&b\\ -a&0&c\\ -b&-c&0 \end{bmatrix} } $$

Therefore,

$$ \boxed{ \frac12(A+A^T)= \begin{bmatrix} 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0 \end{bmatrix} } $$

and

$$ \boxed{ \frac12(A-A^T)= \begin{bmatrix} 0&a&b\\ -a&0&c\\ -b&-c&0 \end{bmatrix} } $$

Question 10

Express the following matrices as the sum of a symmetric and a skew-symmetric matrix.

Solution:

For any square matrix $A$,

$$ A=\frac12(A+A^T)+\frac12(A-A^T), $$

where

  • $\dfrac12(A+A^T)$ is a symmetric matrix.
  • $\dfrac12(A-A^T)$ is a skew-symmetric matrix.

(i)

Given

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 3&5\\ 1&-1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Its transpose is

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 3&1\\ 5&-1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now,

$$ \frac12(A+A^T) = \frac12 \begin{bmatrix} 6&6\\ 6&-2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3&3\\ 3&-1 \end{bmatrix} $$

Also,

$$ \frac12(A-A^T) = \frac12 \begin{bmatrix} 0&4\\ -4&0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0&2\\ -2&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Hence,

$$ \boxed{ \begin{bmatrix} 3&5\\ 1&-1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3&3\\ 3&-1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 0&2\\ -2&0 \end{bmatrix} } $$

(ii)

Given

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 6&-2&2\\ -2&3&-1\\ 2&-1&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 6&-2&2\\ -2&3&-1\\ 2&-1&3 \end{bmatrix} =A, $$

the matrix is already symmetric.

Therefore,

$$ \frac12(A+A^T)=A = \begin{bmatrix} 6&-2&2\\ -2&3&-1\\ 2&-1&3 \end{bmatrix} $$

and

$$ \frac12(A-A^T) = \begin{bmatrix} 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Hence,

$$ \boxed{ \begin{bmatrix} 6&-2&2\\ -2&3&-1\\ 2&-1&3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6&-2&2\\ -2&3&-1\\ 2&-1&3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0\\ 0&0&0 \end{bmatrix} } $$

(iii)

Given

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 3&3&-1\\ -2&-2&1\\ -4&-5&2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Its transpose is

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 3&-2&-4\\ 3&-2&-5\\ -1&1&2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now,

$$ \frac12(A+A^T) = \frac12 \begin{bmatrix} 6&1&-5\\ 1&-4&-4\\ -5&-4&4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3&\dfrac12&-\dfrac52\\ \dfrac12&-2&-2\\ -\dfrac52&-2&2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Also,

$$ \frac12(A-A^T) = \frac12 \begin{bmatrix} 0&5&3\\ -5&0&6\\ -3&-6&0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0&\dfrac52&\dfrac32\\ -\dfrac52&0&3\\ -\dfrac32&-3&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Hence,

$$ \boxed{ \begin{bmatrix} 3&3&-1\\ -2&-2&1\\ -4&-5&2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3&\dfrac12&-\dfrac52\\ \dfrac12&-2&-2\\ -\dfrac52&-2&2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 0&\dfrac52&\dfrac32\\ -\dfrac52&0&3\\ -\dfrac32&-3&0 \end{bmatrix} } $$

(iv)

Given

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} 1&5\\ -1&2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Its transpose is

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 1&-1\\ 5&2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Now,

$$ \frac12(A+A^T) = \frac12 \begin{bmatrix} 2&4\\ 4&4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1&2\\ 2&2 \end{bmatrix} $$

Also,

$$ \frac12(A-A^T) = \frac12 \begin{bmatrix} 0&6\\ -6&0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0&3\\ -3&0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Hence,

$$ \boxed{ \begin{bmatrix} 1&5\\ -1&2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1&2\\ 2&2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 0&3\\ -3&0 \end{bmatrix} } $$

Question 11

If $A$ and $B$ are symmetric matrices of the same order, then $AB-BA$ is a

(A) Skew symmetric matrix
(B) Symmetric matrix
(C) Zero matrix
(D) Identity matrix

Solution:

Since $A$ and $B$ are symmetric matrices,

$$ A^T=A \quad\text{and}\quad B^T=B. $$

Now, take the transpose of $(AB-BA)$.

$$ (AB-BA)^T=(AB)^T-(BA)^T $$

Using the property $(AB)^T=B^TA^T$, we get

$$ (AB-BA)^T=B^TA^T-A^TB^T $$

Since $A^T=A$ and $B^T=B$,

$$ (AB-BA)^T=BA-AB $$ $$ (AB-BA)^T=-(AB-BA) $$

Hence, $AB-BA$ is a skew-symmetric matrix.

Correct Answer: (A) Skew symmetric matrix.

Question 12

If

$$ A= \begin{bmatrix} \cos\alpha&-\sin\alpha\\ \sin\alpha&\cos\alpha \end{bmatrix}, $$

and

$$ A+A^T=I, $$

then the value of $\alpha$ is

(A) $\dfrac{\pi}{6}$
(B) $\dfrac{\pi}{3}$
(C) $\pi$
(D) $\dfrac{3\pi}{2}$

Solution:

First, find the transpose of matrix $A$.

$$ A^T= \begin{bmatrix} \cos\alpha&\sin\alpha\\ -\sin\alpha&\cos\alpha \end{bmatrix} $$

Now, calculate $A+A^T$.

$$ A+A^T= \begin{bmatrix} \cos\alpha&-\sin\alpha\\ \sin\alpha&\cos\alpha \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \cos\alpha&\sin\alpha\\ -\sin\alpha&\cos\alpha \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ A+A^T= \begin{bmatrix} 2\cos\alpha&0\\ 0&2\cos\alpha \end{bmatrix} $$

Since

$$ A+A^T=I= \begin{bmatrix} 1&0\\ 0&1 \end{bmatrix}, $$

equating the corresponding elements, we get

$$ 2\cos\alpha=1 $$ $$ \cos\alpha=\frac12 $$

The value of $\alpha$ satisfying this equation is

$$ \alpha=\frac{\pi}{3}. $$

Correct Answer: (B) $\dfrac{\pi}{3}$.
