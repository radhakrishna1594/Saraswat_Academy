CBSEChapter-8 Questions and activities

Questions and Activities

1. Why do you think people's wants keep changing over time? How does this affect production in an economy? Why cannot all our wants be satisfied?

Answer:

People's wants keep changing over time due to changes in age, income, education, lifestyle, technology, fashion, and social trends. As people's needs and preferences change, they demand new and improved goods and services.

Effect on Production:

  • Producers manufacture new and improved products to meet changing consumer demands.
  • Industries adopt new technologies to improve the quality of goods.
  • Production increases for goods that are in high demand and decreases for those with low demand.
  • New businesses and employment opportunities are created.

Why cannot all our wants be satisfied?

All our wants cannot be satisfied because human wants are unlimited, whereas resources such as land, labour, capital, and time are limited. Since resources are scarce, individuals, businesses, and governments must make choices about how to use them efficiently.

2. "Human wants are unlimited and keep changing." How do you think this constant desire for more creates pressure on the environment? Can the fulfilment of wants and the extraction of resources be balanced?

Answer:

The continuous increase in human wants leads to greater consumption of natural resources. To meet these demands, more forests are cut, minerals are mined, industries are established, and fossil fuels are consumed. This results in environmental problems such as deforestation, pollution, loss of biodiversity, climate change, and depletion of natural resources.

The fulfilment of wants and resource extraction can be balanced by:

  • Using natural resources wisely and sustainably.
  • Promoting renewable sources of energy such as solar and wind energy.
  • Reducing, reusing, and recycling resources.
  • Planting more trees and conserving forests.
  • Encouraging sustainable production and responsible consumption.
  • Creating awareness about environmental conservation.

Thus, economic development and environmental protection can go hand in hand if natural resources are used responsibly and sustainably for present and future generations.

3. Can you think of a resource in your region that is scarce but used wastefully? How could it be managed better?

Answer:

In many parts of India, especially in Rajasthan, water is a scarce resource but is often used wastefully. Water is wasted through leaking pipelines, excessive use in households, flood irrigation in agriculture, and careless consumption.

It can be managed better by:

  • Promoting rainwater harvesting.
  • Repairing leaking taps and pipelines.
  • Using drip and sprinkler irrigation in agriculture.
  • Recycling and reusing water wherever possible.
  • Creating awareness about water conservation.
  • Using water judiciously in homes, industries, and farms.

Proper management of water will help conserve this valuable resource for future generations.

4. Which economic system—market, planned, or mixed—do you think gives people the most freedom? Which economic system is best suited for promoting innovation? Why?

Answer:

The market economy gives people the greatest economic freedom because individuals and businesses are free to choose what to produce, buy, and sell according to demand and supply.

A market economy is also considered the best for promoting innovation because competition encourages producers to develop new products, improve quality, reduce costs, and adopt advanced technologies. Businesses continuously innovate to attract more customers and increase profits.

However, in practice, many countries adopt a mixed economy, where both the government and the private sector work together to promote growth while ensuring social welfare.

5. Critically examine why pure economic systems rarely exist in reality. Assess the limitations of such systems and justify why a mixed economy is often considered a more practical and effective approach in real-world contexts.

Answer:

Pure economic systems, such as a completely market economy or a completely planned economy, rarely exist because each has certain limitations.

Limitations of a Pure Market Economy:

  • Income and wealth inequalities may increase.
  • Essential public services may be neglected.
  • Private firms may exploit consumers or workers.
  • Environmental protection may receive less attention.

Limitations of a Pure Planned Economy:

  • Limited economic freedom for individuals and businesses.
  • Less competition and slower innovation.
  • Inefficient use of resources due to excessive government control.
  • Shortage of goods and services may occur.

Why is a Mixed Economy more practical?

  • It combines the strengths of both market and planned economies.
  • The private sector promotes innovation, efficiency, and competition.
  • The government provides essential public services and social welfare.
  • It helps reduce inequality while encouraging economic growth.
  • It balances economic development with social justice.

Therefore, a mixed economy is considered the most practical system because it combines economic freedom with government regulation and public welfare.

6. A student has ₹100 and must choose between buying a notebook or saving the money for buying a tennis racket later. Which economic concept best explains this situation?

Answer:

Correct Answer: (b) Opportunity Cost

Opportunity cost refers to the value of the next best alternative that is given up when making a choice. In this case, if the student buys the notebook, the opportunity cost is the chance to save the money for buying the tennis racket later.

7. How does understanding opportunity cost improve the quality of economic decision-making?

Answer:

Understanding opportunity cost helps individuals, businesses, and governments make better economic decisions by comparing the benefits and costs of different alternatives before making a choice.

It improves decision-making by:

  • Encouraging careful use of limited resources.
  • Helping people compare different alternatives.
  • Reducing unnecessary expenditure.
  • Promoting better planning and prioritisation.
  • Leading to more efficient and informed decisions.

8. Can effective economic decisions be made without reliable data? Support your answer with an example.

Answer:

No, effective economic decisions cannot be made without reliable data. Accurate information helps governments, businesses, and individuals make informed decisions regarding production, investment, pricing, employment, and resource allocation.

Example:

If a government does not have reliable data on food production, it may fail to estimate the demand for food grains correctly. This can lead to shortages, price rises, or wastage of agricultural produce.

Therefore, reliable data is essential for sound economic planning and decision-making.

9. Analyse how a country's present economic choices can shape its long-term future. Why is it important to consider future consequences while making economic decisions today?

Answer:

The economic choices made today have a significant impact on a country's future development. Decisions regarding education, healthcare, infrastructure, industries, technology, and environmental conservation determine the nation's long-term economic growth and the quality of life of its people.

It is important to consider future consequences because:

  • Resources are limited and should be used sustainably.
  • Wise investments create employment and economic growth.
  • Environmental conservation protects natural resources for future generations.
  • Responsible financial planning reduces future economic problems.
  • Sustainable development ensures balanced economic progress and social welfare.

Thus, economic decisions should focus not only on present needs but also on the welfare of future generations.
