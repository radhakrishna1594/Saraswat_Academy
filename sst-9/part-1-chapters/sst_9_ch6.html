CBSEChapter-6 Questions and activities

Questions and Activities

1. Read the passage and answer the questions that follow:

(a) Which democratic values are reflected in this situation?

Answer:

The democratic values reflected in this situation are:

  • Participation of all members in decision-making.
  • Freedom to express opinions.
  • Equality, as every member gets one vote.
  • Majority rule in making decisions.
  • Respect for different viewpoints.

(b) How does voting help resolve differences of opinion?

Answer:

Voting provides a fair and peaceful way to make decisions when people have different opinions. Every person gets an equal opportunity to express their choice, and the option supported by the majority is accepted. This helps avoid conflicts and ensures that decisions are made democratically.

(c) Why is the majority decision important in a democracy?

Answer:

The majority decision is important because it reflects the opinion of most people. It ensures that decisions are taken fairly after discussion and voting while respecting the rights of those who hold different views. This promotes unity and democratic governance.

(d) Mention responsibilities of elected representatives after a decision is made.

Answer:

  • Implement the decision honestly and efficiently.
  • Work for the welfare of all people.
  • Respect the Constitution and the law.
  • Remain accountable to the citizens.
  • Address public concerns and solve problems fairly.

2. How would democracy be affected if citizens stopped following laws and civic responsibilities? Explain with an example.

Answer:

If citizens stop following laws and civic responsibilities, democracy cannot function effectively. Law and order may break down, public property may be damaged, and people's rights may not be protected. Responsible citizens are essential for the success of democracy.

Example:

If people ignore traffic rules, it may lead to accidents, traffic congestion, and loss of lives. Similarly, if citizens do not vote during elections, they lose the opportunity to choose responsible representatives.

3. Which situation best reflects the idea of popular sovereignty and why?

Answer:

Correct Answer: (b) Citizens vote to choose their representatives.

Popular sovereignty means that the ultimate power in a democracy rests with the people. Citizens exercise this power by electing their representatives through free and fair elections.

4. The Rule of Law is violated when:

Answer:

Correct Answer: (c) Powerful individuals are treated above the law.

The Rule of Law means that everyone is equal before the law, regardless of their position or status. If powerful individuals receive special treatment or are placed above the law, the principle of the Rule of Law is violated.

5. Why is the Rule of Law essential for protecting democracy?

Answer:

The Rule of Law is essential for protecting democracy because it ensures that everyone is equal before the law, regardless of their position, wealth, religion, or social status. It prevents the misuse of power and protects the rights and freedoms of citizens.

Importance of the Rule of Law:

  • Ensures equality before the law.
  • Protects the Fundamental Rights of citizens.
  • Prevents arbitrary use of power by the government.
  • Promotes justice, fairness, and accountability.
  • Strengthens public confidence in democratic institutions.

6. Is voting alone enough to make a country democratic? Support your answer with examples from the chapter.

Answer:

No, voting alone is not enough to make a country democratic. Although free and fair elections are an important feature of democracy, a truly democratic country also requires the Rule of Law, protection of Fundamental Rights, an independent judiciary, accountability of the government, freedom of speech, and active participation of citizens.

Example:

Citizens elect their representatives through voting, but the government must also follow the Constitution, respect people's rights, and remain accountable to the people. Courts should function independently, and citizens should have the freedom to express their opinions peacefully.

7. Social media allows people to express opinions freely.

(a) How can this strengthen democracy?

Answer:

  • It allows citizens to express their opinions freely.
  • It spreads awareness about public issues and government policies.
  • It encourages public participation in democratic processes.
  • It helps people hold governments accountable.
  • It enables quick sharing of important information during emergencies.

(b) How can it weaken democracy if used irresponsibly?

Answer:

  • It can spread fake news and misinformation.
  • It may create hatred and social divisions.
  • False rumours can cause panic and violence.
  • Online abuse and cyberbullying discourage healthy discussions.
  • Misleading information can influence public opinion unfairly.

8. Write a paragraph on what democracy means to you as a young citizen.

Answer:

To me, democracy means living in a country where every citizen has equal rights, freedom, and opportunities. As a young citizen, I believe democracy allows people to express their opinions, respect the views of others, and participate in building a better society. It teaches us to follow the Constitution, obey laws, protect public property, and fulfil our duties responsibly. Democracy also reminds us that every citizen has an important role in making the nation strong, peaceful, and inclusive.

10. Choose any one democratic institution in India (such as Parliament, Election Commission, Judiciary, or Panchayat). Prepare a short note explaining:

Answer:

Democratic Institution Chosen: Election Commission of India

(a) Its role in democracy:

  • Conducts free and fair elections.
  • Prepares and updates electoral rolls.
  • Ensures that elections are conducted according to the Constitution.
  • Monitors political parties and election campaigns.

(b) How it ensures accountability and participation:

  • Provides every eligible citizen with the opportunity to vote.
  • Ensures equal opportunities for all political parties.
  • Enforces the Model Code of Conduct during elections.
  • Promotes transparent and impartial elections.

11. What role does the Constitution play in strengthening democracy in India?

Answer:

The Constitution is the supreme law of India and forms the foundation of Indian democracy. It defines the powers and responsibilities of the government and guarantees Fundamental Rights to all citizens.

It strengthens democracy by:

  • Protecting the rights and freedoms of citizens.
  • Ensuring equality before the law.
  • Providing a framework for democratic governance.
  • Maintaining the separation of powers among the Legislature, Executive, and Judiciary.
  • Promoting justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity.

12. What challenges do you think democracy in India faces today?

Answer:

India's democracy has achieved significant progress, but it also faces several challenges.

  • Corruption and misuse of public funds.
  • Spread of fake news and misinformation.
  • Low political awareness among some citizens.
  • Social inequality and discrimination.
  • Influence of money and criminal elements in elections.
  • Communalism and social conflicts.
  • Need for greater transparency and accountability in governance.

These challenges can be addressed through quality education, responsible citizenship, transparent governance, strong democratic institutions, and active public participation.
