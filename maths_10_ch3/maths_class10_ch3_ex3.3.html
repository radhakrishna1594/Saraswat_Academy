EXERCISE 3.3

Question 1: Solve the following pair of linear equations by the Elimination Method and the Substitution Method

(i) x + y = 5    and    2x − 3y = 4

▶ Elimination Method

Given equations:

Equation (1):   x + y = 5
Equation (2):   2x − 3y = 4

Step 1: Multiply Equation (1) by 2 to make the coefficient of x equal in both equations:

2 × (x + y = 5)  ⇒  2x + 2y = 10    ... (3)

Step 2: Subtract Equation (2) from Equation (3):

(2x + 2y) − (2x − 3y) = 10 − 4
2x + 2y − 2x + 3y = 6
5y = 6
y = 6/5

Step 3: Substitute y = 6/5 into Equation (1):

x + 6/5 = 5
x = 5 − 6/5
x = 25/5 − 6/5
x = 19/5

Solution by Elimination: x = 19/5, y = 6/5

▶ Substitution Method

Step 1: From Equation (1), express x in terms of y:

x = 5 − y    ... (3)

Step 2: Substitute into Equation (2):

2(5 − y) − 3y = 4
10 − 2y − 3y = 4
10 − 5y = 4
−5y = 4 − 10
−5y = −6
y = 6/5

Step 3: Substitute y = 6/5 back into (3):

x = 5 − 6/5 = 25/5 − 6/5 = 19/5

Solution by Substitution: x = 19/5, y = 6/5

(ii) 3x + 4y = 10    and    2x − 2y = 2

▶ Elimination Method

Given equations:

Equation (1):   3x + 4y = 10
Equation (2):   2x − 2y = 2

Step 1: Multiply Equation (2) by 2 to make the coefficient of y equal in magnitude:

2 × (2x − 2y = 2)  ⇒  4x − 4y = 4    ... (3)

Step 2: Add Equation (1) and Equation (3) to eliminate y:

(3x + 4y) + (4x − 4y) = 10 + 4
7x = 14
x = 2

Step 3: Substitute x = 2 into Equation (2):

2(2) − 2y = 2
4 − 2y = 2
−2y = −2
y = 1

Solution by Elimination: x = 2, y = 1

Substitution Method

Step 1: From Equation (2), simplify and express x in terms of y:

2x − 2y = 2
x − y = 1
x = 1 + y    ... (3)

Step 2: Substitute into Equation (1):

3(1 + y) + 4y = 10
3 + 3y + 4y = 10
7y = 7
y = 1

Step 3: Substitute y = 1 into (3):

x = 1 + 1 = 2

Solution by Substitution: x = 2, y = 1

(iii) 3x − 5y − 4 = 0    and    9x = 2y + 7

▶ Elimination Method

Rewrite in standard form:

Equation (1):   3x − 5y = 4
Equation (2):   9x − 2y = 7

Step 1: Multiply Equation (1) by 3 to make the coefficient of x equal:

3 × (3x − 5y = 4)  ⇒  9x − 15y = 12    ... (3)

Step 2: Subtract Equation (2) from Equation (3):

(9x − 15y) − (9x − 2y) = 12 − 7
9x − 15y − 9x + 2y = 5
−13y = 5
y = −5/13

Step 3: Substitute y = −5/13 into Equation (1):

3x − 5(−5/13) = 4
3x + 25/13 = 4
3x = 4 − 25/13
3x = 52/13 − 25/13
3x = 27/13
x = 27/(13 × 3)
x = 9/13

Solution by Elimination: x = 9/13, y = −5/13

Substitution Method

Step 1: From Equation (1), express x in terms of y:

3x = 4 + 5y
x = (4 + 5y) / 3    ... (3)

Step 2: Substitute into Equation (2): 9x − 2y = 7

9 × [(4 + 5y) / 3] − 2y = 7
3(4 + 5y) − 2y = 7
12 + 15y − 2y = 7
13y = 7 − 12
13y = −5
y = −5/13

Step 3: Substitute y = −5/13 into (3):

x = (4 + 5 × (−5/13)) / 3
x = (4 − 25/13) / 3
x = (52/13 − 25/13) / 3
x = (27/13) / 3
x = 27 / 39
x = 9/13

Solution by Substitution: x = 9/13, y = −5/13

(iv) x/2 + 2y/3 = −1    and    x − y/3 = 3

▶ Elimination Method

Step 1: Clear the fractions. Multiply Equation (1) by 6 and Equation (2) by 3:

6 × (x/2 + 2y/3 = −1)  ⇒  3x + 4y = −6    ... (A)
3 × (x − y/3 = 3)  ⇒  3x − y = 9    ... (B)

Step 2: Subtract Equation (B) from Equation (A) to eliminate x:

(3x + 4y) − (3x − y) = −6 − 9
3x + 4y − 3x + y = −15
5y = −15
y = −3

Step 3: Substitute y = −3 into Equation (B):

3x − (−3) = 9
3x + 3 = 9
3x = 6
x = 2

Solution by Elimination: x = 2, y = −3

Substitution Method

Step 1: From Equation (B): 3x − y = 9, express y in terms of x:

y = 3x − 9    ... (C)

Step 2: Substitute into Equation (A): 3x + 4y = −6

3x + 4(3x − 9) = −6
3x + 12x − 36 = −6
15x = −6 + 36
15x = 30
x = 2

Step 3: Substitute x = 2 into (C):

y = 3(2) − 9 = 6 − 9 = −3

Solution by Substitution: x = 2, y = −3

Question 2: Form the pair of linear equations and find their solutions by the Elimination Method

(i) If we add 1 to the numerator and subtract 1 from the denominator, the fraction reduces to 1. It becomes 1/2 if we only add 1 to the denominator. What is the fraction?

▶ Step 1: Define Variables

Let the numerator = x
Let the denominator = y
So the fraction = x / y

▶ Step 2: Form the Equations

Condition 1: Add 1 to numerator and subtract 1 from denominator → fraction becomes 1:

(x + 1) / (y − 1) = 1
x + 1 = y − 1
x − y = −2    ... (1)

Condition 2: Add 1 to denominator only → fraction becomes 1/2:

x / (y + 1) = 1/2
2x = y + 1
2x − y = 1    ... (2)

▶ Step 3: Solve by Elimination Method

Subtract Equation (1) from Equation (2):

(2x − y) − (x − y) = 1 − (−2)
2x − y − x + y = 3
x = 3

Substitute x = 3 into Equation (1):

3 − y = −2
−y = −5
y = 5

▶ Verification

Fraction = 3/5
Condition 1: (3+1)/(5−1) = 4/4 = 1 ✓
Condition 2: 3/(5+1) = 3/6 = 1/2 ✓

✓ The fraction is 3/5

(ii) Five years ago, Nuri was thrice as old as Sonu. Ten years later, Nuri will be twice as old as Sonu. Find the present ages of Nuri and Sonu.

▶ Step 1: Define Variables

Let Nuri's present age = x years
Let Sonu's present age = y years

▶ Step 2: Form the Equations

Condition 1: Five years ago, Nuri was thrice as old as Sonu:

(x − 5) = 3(y − 5)
x − 5 = 3y − 15
x − 3y = −10    ... (1)

Condition 2: Ten years later, Nuri will be twice as old as Sonu:

(x + 10) = 2(y + 10)
x + 10 = 2y + 20
x − 2y = 10    ... (2)

▶ Step 3: Solve by Elimination Method

Subtract Equation (1) from Equation (2):

(x − 2y) − (x − 3y) = 10 − (−10)
x − 2y − x + 3y = 20
y = 20

Substitute y = 20 into Equation (2):

x − 2(20) = 10
x − 40 = 10
x = 50

▶ Verification

Present ages: Nuri = 50, Sonu = 20
Five years ago: Nuri = 45, Sonu = 15 → 45 = 3 × 15 ✓
Ten years later: Nuri = 60, Sonu = 30 → 60 = 2 × 30 ✓

✓ Nuri's present age = 50 years, Sonu's present age = 20 years

(iii) The sum of the digits of a two-digit number is 9.
Nine times this number is twice the number obtained by reversing the order of digits.
Find the number.

▶ Step 1: Define Variables

Let the tens digit = x
Let the units digit = y
So the original number = 10x + y
The reversed number = 10y + x

▶ Step 2: Form the Equations

Condition 1: Sum of digits is 9:

x + y = 9    ... (1)

Condition 2: Nine times the number equals twice the reversed number:

9 × (10x + y) = 2 × (10y + x)
90x + 9y = 20y + 2x
90x − 2x + 9y − 20y = 0
88x − 11y = 0
Divide throughout by 11:
8x − y = 0    ... (2)

▶ Step 3: Solve by Elimination Method

Add Equation (1) and Equation (2):

(x + y) + (8x − y) = 9 + 0
9x = 9
x = 1

Substitute x = 1 into Equation (1):

1 + y = 9
y = 8

▶ Verification

Original number = 10(1) + 8 = 18
Reversed number = 10(8) + 1 = 81
Condition 1: 1 + 8 = 9 ✓
Condition 2: 9 × 18 = 162  and  2 × 81 = 162 ✓

✓ The two-digit number is 18

(iv) Meena went to a bank to withdraw ₹2000. She asked the cashier to give her ₹50 and ₹100 notes only. Meena got 25 notes in all. Find how many notes of ₹50 and ₹100 she received.

Solution:

Let the number of ₹50 notes be x.

Let the number of ₹100 notes be y.

According to the question, Meena received 25 notes in all.

Therefore,

x + y = 25    ...(1)

The total amount withdrawn was ₹2000.

Therefore,

50x + 100y = 2000

Dividing the whole equation by 50, we get

x + 2y = 40    ...(2)

Subtract equation (1) from equation (2).

(x + 2y) - (x + y) = 40 - 25

x + 2y - x - y = 15

y = 15

Substitute y = 15 in equation (1).

x + 15 = 25

x = 10

Verification:

10 × 50 = ₹500

15 × 100 = ₹1500

Total amount = ₹500 + ₹1500 = ₹2000 ✓

Answer:

Meena received 10 notes of ₹50 and 15 notes of ₹100.

(v) A lending library has a fixed charge for the first three days and an additional charge for each day thereafter. Saritha paid ₹27 for a book kept for seven days, while Susy paid ₹21 for the book she kept for five days. Find the fixed charge and the charge for each extra day.

Solution:

Let the fixed charge for the first three days be ₹x.

Let the additional charge for each extra day be ₹y.

For Saritha:

She kept the book for 7 days.

Extra days = 7 - 3 = 4 days

Total amount paid = ₹27

Therefore,

x + 4y = 27    ...(1)

For Susy:

She kept the book for 5 days.

Extra days = 5 - 3 = 2 days

Total amount paid = ₹21

Therefore,

x + 2y = 21    ...(2)

Subtract equation (2) from equation (1).

(x + 4y) - (x + 2y) = 27 - 21

x + 4y - x - 2y = 6

2y = 6

y = 3

Substitute y = 3 in equation (2).

x + 2(3) = 21

x + 6 = 21

x = 15

Verification:

For 7 days:

₹15 + (4 × ₹3) = ₹15 + ₹12 = ₹27 ✓

For 5 days:

₹15 + (2 × ₹3) = ₹15 + ₹6 = ₹21 ✓

Answer:

Fixed charge for the first three days = ₹15

Charge for each extra day = ₹3 per day
